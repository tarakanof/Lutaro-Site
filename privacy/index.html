Lutaro Privacy Policy

Effective 2 July 2026

Lutaro is an app for managing Sony camera Picture Profiles on iPhone, iPad, and Mac. The short version: Lutaro collects no data about you. There are no accounts, no analytics, no tracking, no ads, and no servers of ours — nothing you do in the app is sent to us or to any third party.

Data we collect

None. Lutaro has no analytics or telemetry of any kind and never phones home.

Your profile library

The Picture Profiles, tags, folders, and preview images you create are stored on your device. If you enable iCloud on your device, the library can sync through your own iCloud account (Apple's iCloud Documents); that data is handled by Apple under Apple's privacy policy and is never visible to us.

Camera connections and credentials

Lutaro talks directly to your Sony camera over your local Wi-Fi network or USB. Camera passwords are stored in the device Keychain; connection metadata (such as a camera's name and identifiers) is stored on your device. Credentials are sent only to your camera during connection — never anywhere else.

Local network access

The app asks for local-network permission solely to discover and connect to your Sony camera on your Wi-Fi network. It does not scan for, connect to, or collect information about any other devices.

Photo library access

If you choose a sample image for a profile, the app reads only the photo you pick. It never browses or uploads your photo library.

Diagnostics

Lutaro can produce a diagnostics report to help troubleshoot camera connections. Reports are created only when you ask for one, are redacted by default (network addresses and serial numbers removed), and never leave your device unless you export and share them yourself.

Changes

If this policy changes materially, the updated version will be published at this address with a new effective date.
